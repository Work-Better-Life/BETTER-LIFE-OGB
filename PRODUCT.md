# Product

## Register

product

## Users

A single teacher/administrator who records student test scores and reviews them afterward — sometimes right after marking a stack of tests (fast data entry, phone or laptop), sometimes during a quiet weekly review looking for who's improving and who's slipping (slower, exploratory, desktop-favored). There is no second user role: no parents, no students, no co-teachers logging in.

## Product Purpose

Turn raw per-topic test scores into visible trends over time. The job isn't "store scores" — spreadsheets already do that — it's "tell me, at a glance, who's improving, who's consistent, and who needs attention," without the overhead of a full school-management system (no fees, attendance, HR, or multi-role permissions).

## Brand Personality

Encouraging, clear, warm. This is a tool a teacher uses to notice and celebrate progress, not a compliance/reporting instrument. Deltas and trends should read as supportive signal ("this student is climbing") rather than clinical measurement. Never cold, never corporate, never alarmist about dips — framed as "worth a look," not "failing."

## Anti-references

- The cluttered, multi-module school-ERP dashboards in the reference screenshots this project drew from (fee tracking, HR, attendance, family/guardian portals) — this app is deliberately smaller than those, and should never grow visual density to match them.
- Generic AI-SaaS analytics dashboards: gradient hero-metric tiles, identical icon-in-a-box card grids, glassmorphism panels. Cold and interchangeable with any other B2B tool.
- Clinical/enterprise admin panels (dense data-grid-everything, no warmth, gray-on-gray).

## Design Principles

- Surface the trend, not just the number — a bare score without its history/delta is an incomplete answer to what this app is for.
- One primary action per screen (add a score, add a student) — no competing CTAs.
- Small-dataset honesty — this tracks one classroom's worth of data, not enterprise scale; no false urgency, no dashboards that look empty until "at scale."
- Constructive framing over clinical measurement — color, copy, and emphasis should read as encouragement, even when a score dips.
- Quiet confidence over spectacle — calm surfaces most of the time, with deliberate, earned moments of color/motion (a genuine improvement, a milestone) rather than constant visual noise.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Standard WCAG AA: solid contrast ratios in both light and dark themes, full keyboard navigation, visible focus states, respects `prefers-reduced-motion`.
